Backstory

My name is Hunter Allen Tulius, cool initials right, I was born in 2003 in la crosse WI and lived the first half of my life in Independence WI population 1,477. My mother was very young when she had me, so I was raised by my grandparents until my mom was ready to take care of me. Freshman year of high school I moved in with my mom in Whitehall WI, a town just slightly bigger. After high school I got accepted to UT Dallas, but it was too expensive to go, so I fell back on my second choice UW Madison, but again couldn’t afford it. Luckily UW Eau Claire was still taking application in mid-august, and I quickly got in, and I now I love it here.

Passions & Goals

I don’t have much I’m really passionate about but the few things I am, I really am. I absolutely love video games and I make sure to find any time I can to sit down and play a few hours. I also love the news, particularly on the ground reporting, which is my dream job, especially a war correspondent. Videography goes with that heavily and so I’ve been getting more and more into those types of mediums. My goal is to get into some sort of news position by the time I graduate and just work my way to that dream eventually.

Education

My high school wasn’t the best having no more than 40 kids in a grade doesn’t lead to the best funding. Independence High was able to give me a fairly good education, however. The school worked really hard to give its students the best and got really creative with how they reached those goals. I learned a lot from some of the best teachers I know, all because they cared deeply about the students and wanted to see us succeed. I hope to go back there some day as an alumnus. My college education I would say is very similar, UWEC isn’t the biggest school out their, but they know that and don’t try to act like they’re the best. The school focuses on what’s best for students and it really shows in the education and other experiences of the school. I don’t regret either of these schools as my choice and would do pick both again in a heartbeat.

Personally, I don’t think I need physical awards to know my strengths and if others, whether it’s a job of a school, don’t trust me they can find someone else.
